Planner Overview

A fully integrated, multi-platform content plan built to move the audience from awareness into understanding, then into hope, then into action.

How to use this plannerUse weekly overview blocks to guide theme, tone, and CTA intensity. Use the daily cards for execution-level planning and production handoff.
Planner logicThis is a progressive content system, not a collection of isolated posts. Lower-friction asks appear first. More direct action prompts show up after context and credibility are established.
Content principlesClarity over jargon. Human tone over institutional tone. Visual simplicity over clutter. Educational value over filler.
Execution note: Timing windows should guide publishing, not dictate it rigidly. Prioritize relevance, campaign context, and real performance signals over blind adherence to schedule.

January Signals

Federal Holidays

Federal Holidays (U.S.)

These are the dates most likely to affect business operations, scheduling, and social performance. Expect lower engagement around New Year’s Day and the usual long-weekend slowdown around MLK Day.

  • January 1 — New Year’s Day
  • January 19 — Martin Luther King Jr. Day
Awareness

High-Value Awareness Days

These are strong credibility-building moments, especially for mission-driven brands. They tend to work well for educational content, thoughtful storytelling, and brand-aligned awareness posts.

Month-Long Observances

  • Dry January
  • National Mentoring Month
  • Mental Wellness Month

Key Dates

  • January 4 — World Braille Day
  • January 11 — Human Trafficking Awareness Day
  • January 13 — Korean American Day
  • January 19 — Martin Luther King Jr. Day
  • January 20 — Inauguration Day (every four years)
  • January 24 — International Day of Education
  • January 27 — International Holocaust Remembrance Day
Engagement

High-Engagement Days

These are lighter, more shareable moments that can help drive reach and interaction. Good for visibility. Not where you build authority.

  • January 2 — National Science Fiction Day
  • January 5 — National Bird Day
  • January 10 — Houseplant Appreciation Day
  • January 14 — Dress Up Your Pet Day
  • January 21 — National Hugging Day
  • January 23 — National Pie Day
  • January 24 — Compliment Day
  • January 29 — Puzzle Day
Notes

Notes

Use January to lead with grounded optimism rather than generic reset language. Dry January references should stay practical and stigma-safe. MLK content should only appear where it is directly tied to mission and values. Mid-month is the best time to increase save-worthy educational content as motivation drops. Late January is the right moment to pivot messaging toward sustainability, systems, and long-term change.

Content Opportunities

Use these themes to shape January content in a way that feels timely, intentional, and actually useful.

Jan 1–10

1. Reset Window

The natural opening for content built around fresh starts, momentum, and realistic forward movement.

  • Behavior change
  • Goal setting
  • Systems over motivation
Mid-January

2. Mid-Month Drop-Off

Motivation starts fading here. This is where more honest, contrarian, truth-based content tends to land.

  • Resolution fatigue
  • Accountability
  • Messaging that cuts through the fluff
MLK Window

3. Purpose + Impact

Best approached through substance, not performance. Values-based storytelling only works when it is genuinely tied to the mission.

  • Social impact storytelling
  • Mission alignment
  • Values-led messaging with depth
Late January

4. Long-Term Sustainability

A strong window for shifting the conversation away from hype and toward endurance, systems, and actual change.

  • Long-term habits
  • Systems thinking
  • Real change over quick wins

Common Misfires

  • Generic “New Year, New Me” messaging
  • Performative MLK content with no real tie to your brand or mission
  • Random national-day posts that add noise but no value
